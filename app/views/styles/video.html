Notes

The video team use a product called brightcove to serve video content on the NHS.UK site, so we were limited in how much we could edit the player - not able to include custom play icons etc.

Also, due to the way video content has been rolled out - we were unable to make sweeping changes across the site in one go. Videos will need to be updated individually.

The video team is currently in discovery for a new video player, we'll be passing our findings and working closely with the team to ensure our initial learnings are built on.

User needs

  • As a user I WANT visual context (supporting written information), SO THAT I can help identify my health thing (condition / symptom / medicine).
  • As a user I WANT an alternative to text, SO THAT I can seen how to do something.

Whats currently being used

  • We have a mixture of educational health videos such as La Bomba dance exercise https://www.nhs.uk/conditions/nhs-fitness-studio/dance-la-bomba/
  • and informative videos - such as Sore throat https://www.nhs.uk/conditions/sore-throat/

Our hypothesis

  • Users want to know how long the video is upfront
  • Users don't understand review dates
  • Users don't want overlaid text (disapears when you press play)

Research findings

  • 3/5 users recognised the video component and were easily able to use it
  • However, one user thought the video would link to another page rather than play inline
  • Another user, who had a visual and cognitive impairment, didn’t recognise the video on the page
  • All users commented the video was helpful

NHS.UK usability lab London, 25th July 2018

Example

Accessibility

We made the player background black and the icons white which meets AAA WCAG standards.

We also increased the size of the icons - so they have a larger hit area.

The title and description has moved from being overlaid the video content to beneath the player and we've introduced the duration of the video.

For further reading:

  • Accessibility Oz: Video factsheet https://www.accessibilityoz.com/factsheets/video/video-factsheet/
  • Accessibility Oz: Video accessibility https://www.accessibilityoz.com/services/video-accessibility/
  • Harvard University: Accessible media player https://accessibility.huit.harvard.edu/provide-accessible-media-player
  • NNGroup: video usability https://www.nngroup.com/articles/video-usability/

Usage

  • Only use video media where it adds to user understanding
  • Don't be overly reliant on video to convey information - from an accessibility perspective, providing content as a video can limit access to the information contained in this format for anyone who cannot see or hear the content. In addition, videos break. In testing we’ve seen many a user run across a video that won’t load, doesn’t appear, can’t be played, or freezes. (https://www.nngroup.com/articles/video-usability/)
  • Video sizing - to ensure video flows with content; size 2/3 on larger screens (desktop / tablet) and full width on small screens (mobile)
  • Title and description - do not overlay video, must sit within content.
  • Clearly display the length of the video
  • Be upfront with users if the video contains graphic content

Going forward

What is success

Success would be an increase in plays and increase in users watching the content till the end.

How we measure success

Success could be measured by plays and retention (users watching video content until the end).

Hypotheses for design iterations

We'd like to explore the use of our own icons - for constancy across the site. Having the play bar always visible (on the bright cove player it only appears when you hover the mouse).

Also working are on the media review dates, which have place on the page (to meet the NHS England info standard) - but don't meet any user needs.
