The NHS login

The NHS login is a safe and convenient single point of access to approved health websites and apps. It’s been introduced to empower patients and lower the burden on frontline NHS health professionals.

At first, it will be used to access the NHS App. The number of websites and apps that can use the NHS login with will increase over time.

The NHS login allows people to set up an online account using their email address and mobile phone. Their identity is then authenticated and verified using a trusted photo ID document such as a passport or driving licence plus a short video of themselves.

Once people have proved who they are with photo ID and video, they can use their single NHS login repeatedly without having to prove who they are each time.

It's based on the government's Identity Proofing and Verification of an Individual good practice guide. All services that use our authentication and verification platform must meet NHS Digital's standard.

Which customer journeys are supported by the NHS login service

Customer journeys currently supported by the NHS login service are:

  • account creation
  • identity verification
  • basic account management (changing password, email address and phone number)

At the moment the service only covers customers in England and those with a British passport or UK driving license.

Which customer journeys are not supported

At the moment the service does not cover:

  • customers who want to re-use their Patient Online (POL) verification
  • customers who want to use offline methods of verification, for example post office or health professional vouching of identity
